The Curriculum

What You Will Learn

Every session takes place inside our working studio in Ile-Ife, surrounded by real indigo vats, authentic fabrics and master artisans.

01

The History of Adire

Understand where Adire came from — its Yoruba roots, its role in cultural identity and how it evolved from a village craft into a globally recognised art form.

02

Tools, Materials & Dyes

Learn every tool by name and purpose. Understand the chemistry of natural indigo versus synthetic dyes, how to prepare a dye vat and achieve consistent colour.

03

Adire Alabere (Stitch Resist)

Master the needle-and-thread technique — stitching patterns into fabric before dyeing to create complex geometric and organic designs that are unique to each piece.

04

Adire Eleko (Paste Resist)

Learn to mix and apply cassava starch paste using the traditional wooden comb (obi) and freehand painting to block dye penetration and create signature patterns.

05

Tie & Dye Techniques

Explore folding, twisting, binding and clamping methods — each producing its own distinctive burst pattern. Develop your own signature style through repetition and experimentation.

06

Finishing & Fabric Care

Learn the correct washing, drying and pressing process that preserves colour depth and extends the life of your fabric. Understand how to package and present finished pieces.

07

Building Your Own Adire Business

From pricing your work to finding buyers — we share what we have learned running Ronaks, including how to use social media and WhatsApp to sell premium Adire.

08

Mentorship & Ongoing Support

Your relationship with Ronaks does not end when training ends. Graduate students get access to our WhatsApp mentorship group, material sourcing support and feedback on their work.
